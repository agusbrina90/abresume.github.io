Dictalex a LegalTech/AI project

AI - Product Management - Market Strategy

From Concept to a $100M COP Contract in 14 Months

The problem:

Colombia’s legal sector faced rising demand but was constrained by manual transcription—one hour of audio required two to three hours of work. This bottleneck caused delays and diverted highly skilled professionals from strategic legal tasks to low-value administrative work.

The vision:

To streamline the judicial workflow in Colombia by providing an adaptive AI solution that transforms document management, freeing legal professionals from repetitive administrative work to focus on high-value legal strategy.

The solution:

We developed Dictalex, an AI-powered platform engineered specifically for the legal sector. It transforms audio and video into precise, legally-specific text and includes a suite of powerful features designed to optimize the entire documentation workflow, such as bulk file upload, advanced speaker identification, and sensitive information anonymization.


My Role & Key Contributions:
  • Maximizing the product's value by defining the vision, managing the backlog, and translating feedback from 15+ legal professionals into actionable user stories. To ensure predictable delivery, we used the Fibonacci sequence for story point estimation to accurately forecast our team's velocity.

  • The team and process: I directed the go-to-market strategy, managed the budget, and facilitated all agile ceremonies (sprint planning, stand-ups, retrospectives). A key part of my role was to protect the team from scope creep and remove impediments, ensuring they could maintain a sustainable and productive pace.

  • Contributed to front-end development, shaped the brand and communication strategy, conducted foundational market research, created all user documentation, and designed the automated Intercom customer service flow based on documentation data.

Lessons learned:
  • Navigating Crisis with a Strategic Pivot: An early setback forced a strategic realignment. I led the data-driven decision to pivot from a high-investment enterprise solution to an MVP-first approach. This was crucial for our success, allowing us to get to market faster, validate assumptions with less risk, and build a strong foundation before scaling.

  • The Art of Managing Expectations and Scope: A key challenge was handling a stakeholder pushing for constant new features. Through negotiation and transparent communication, I safeguarded team focus, balanced stakeholder needs, and ensured only essential MVP features were prioritized within limited resources.

  • Building a Proactive "Challenge Culture": Early on, team members hesitated to question ideas. As facilitator, I fostered a culture of proactive communication where constructive challenges were welcomed, strengthening collaboration, improving processes, and ultimately enhancing the final product.
