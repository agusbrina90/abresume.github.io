Growth Hacking & Marketing

Research – Automation – Growth

Driving 187% Audience Growth and 1M+ Viral Views Through Strategic Content

The challenge:

The NGO, Proyecto Periquito, had a powerful mission of fostering intergenerational connections but struggled to scale its impact and reach new audiences. Their their social media presence was not yet optimized for growth, leaving significant potential untapped. They needed a strategic overhaul to increase their visibility, engagement, and ultimately, their ability to attract volunteers and donations.

How did we do it?
  • Management & Systems: I designed and implemented a 28-day data-driven cycle that included one strategic phase and four weekly execution sprints. The workflow began with a 3-day monthly social media analysis and strategy of all platforms to ideation, scripting, recording events, editing, stakeholder approval and transparent documentation focusing in results and velocity.

  • Content Strategy & Growth Hacking: I developed and executed a data-driven content strategy based on a methodology of "Research and Iteration". The strategy began with a deep-dive analysis to understand our audience and competitors’ audiences (direct/indirect) which led us to identify how to boost our engagement. This research was used to build a "Content Warchest" of proven ideas with compelling narratives and strong hooks, telling our story in a way that kept us relevant and led to organic growth.

  • Automation & Innovation: Created an automated publishing pipeline using standardized file management and scheduling to ensure consistent and efficient content deployment. The primary goal was to automate publishing wherever possible, ensuring a consistent and efficient content delivery pipeline. This system allowed us to manage content deployment efficiently, which supported our engagement rates.

Lessons learned:
  • Balancing Volunteer Availability with System Demands:Many volunteers were engaged in other projects, making it difficult to maintain consistency. We solved this by creating a flexible system that respected availability while still ensuring coverage across the entire marketing workflow.Lesson: Scalability requires not just systems but human-centered design that keeps volunteers motivated.

  • Building a Repeatable Content Cycle:Early efforts were ad-hoc and reactive, which limited our reach. By formalizing a 4-week cycle—covering research, ideation, scripting, batch recording, editing, approval, publishing, and iteration—we brought structure and velocity to content creation. Lesson: Processes are essential to sustain creativity at scale.

  • Continuous Iteration as a Growth EngineEven after fixes, issues surfaced. By embedding iteration into the cycle, we ensured that improvements were ongoing rather than one-time. Lesson: Sustainable growth requires treating every campaign as a live experiment.
