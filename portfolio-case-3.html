High-Stakes Event Project Management

Digital infrastructure – Marketing – Logistics

Orchestrating a 337% Increase in Attendance for a National Congress

The challenge:

The primary objective for the "II Congreso Nacional de Derecho Disciplinario" was to dramatically increase attendance and ticket sales compared to the previous year. This required a comprehensive project management approach, encompassing everything from digital marketing and web development to complex, on-the-ground logistics.

My Role: End-to-End Project Management & Operations
  • Digital Marketing Strategy: I orchestrated a multi-channel digital marketing strategy that was the primary driver of ticket sales. This included developing a new event landing page, managing SEO, and executing targeted ad campaigns across Google Ads, Facebook Ads, and email marketing.

  • Logistical Coordination: I managed the end-to-end event logistics for the 350-attendee congress. This was a significant operational challenge that involved coordinating 20+ speakers, managing 7+ vendors (including venue, catering, and technical support), and securing two high-profile partnerships.

  • Timeline and Deliverable Management: I developed and managed a master project plan and timeline, ensuring that all deliverables—from digital assets and ad campaigns to physical materials and vendor contracts—were completed on schedule and within budget.
Key Quantitative Results:
Metric Result Impact
Attendance Growth ≈312% increase More than tripled the number of attendees from the previous year.
Ticket Sales Generation ≈86% The digital marketing strategy I managed generated the vast majority of all ticket sales.

Lessons learned:
  • Navigating the "Chicken-and-Egg" Budget Problem: The project faced a budget cycle problem: ticket sales were needed to fund marketing, yet marketing was essential to drive those sales. I negotiated an initial budget allocation from stakeholders to break this impasse. This experience underscored the value of data-driven marketing, where every dollar had to be optimized through rigorous analysis and strategic execution to ensure campaigns generated the resources needed to scale.

  • Managing Risk in High-Growth Scenarios: Expanding attendance from 80 to a target of 350 in a new city created logistical and financial risks. To manage uncertainty, I applied agile vendor management, negotiating flexible arrangements with venues, catering, and suppliers. This allowed us to scale responsibly, adapt to fluctuating demand, and maintain quality without overcommitting resources.
